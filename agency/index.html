The honest answers.

Does it produce actual creatives, or just the intel?

The MCP is the intel layer: what's winning in a category, why it works, and what to build. It gives your team the pattern and the hook to brief against.

Creative production (AI video and static) is a separate Orcool offering. The intel layer ships first, because that's where most teams stall.

How does multi-brand work? Do I pay per client?

One Orcool account. You add each client as a brand inside the platform. Each brand has its own competitor set, category signals, and Slack channel.

Pricing covers your agency seat, not individual brands. Talk to us about what that looks like at your scale.

Can concept cards stream to Slack on a daily cadence?

Yes. The Slack integration delivers daily brand alerts, pattern cards, and weekly reports per brand. You set the cadence and the channel.

A structured concept stream (three ideas per day per client) is part of the production offering, not the free trial. Start with the intel layer first and see what the signal looks like.

What do I get in the first five minutes?

Set up your first brand, paste in three to five competitors, and Orcool pulls their recent ads and returns the first category read: what's running, what structure is working, and a hook to brief against.

From there you can add more brands or ask follow-on questions in the same session.

Where does the data come from? How fresh is it?

Competitor ads are pulled from Meta Ad Library and refreshed continuously. Pattern data updates daily. You're seeing what's actually running, not a snapshot from last quarter.

Do I need to install anything?

No. Paste the MCP URL into Claude, ChatGPT, or Cursor. Sign in with the magic link. That's it.

Why not just ask Claude or ChatGPT without Orcool?

Claude on its own knows nothing about what's actually running in your client's category right now. It reasons from training data, which lags months. Orcool pulls live competitor ads and surfaces patterns from what's actually in market today. The signal is real, not inferred.
