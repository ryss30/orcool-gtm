The shift

Edtech's creative cycle is squeezed by the calendar, not the algorithm.

Back-to-school, the summer-learning-loss push, and the January resolution spike for language apps aren't marketing themes, they're most of the year's outcome decided inside a few compressed weeks. Miss the window on a concept that never earns real permission from parents and platforms, and you're not waiting a week for the next shot, you're waiting for next season. Meanwhile most of the category keeps running the same clip: a kid smiling at a tablet, a streak counter ticking up. Parents have seen it, platforms have seen it, and it converts a little less every month it stays live.

So the number that matters isn't how many variants you can push out before the window closes. It's the calendar days from starting a cycle to a creative that clears your KPI gate and still holds once the season's traffic settles. We call that Time-to-Winner, and we only count it paired with durability. A safe winner that fades the week back-to-school ends doesn't make the count.

A language-learning app spending its whole January budget on five versions of the same "new year, new habit" hook, all live before day ten, all fighting for the same feed.

Questions

The honest answers.

Do I need to be an engineer?

No. Read the feeds in Slack and there's nothing to build. The MCP is for wiring the signal into your own systems. Plenty of producers start in Slack and move over later.

How does sign-in work?

Paste the MCP URL into your client. It opens a browser tab, you enter your email, and we send a one-click magic link. No password, no separate account to set up. After that the tools are live in every conversation.

Is the data real, or model guesswork?

Real. We read the ad libraries, the app stores, and the press directly, and a human checks the read before it reaches you. The honest caveat for edtech: platforms review youth-targeted ad creative more heavily than most categories, and COPPA-adjacent restrictions mean some competitors run a thinner public library than their actual spend would suggest. What is visible, we analyze closely, and we pair it with app-store and press signals so a thin library doesn't leave you flying blind. When something is an inference rather than an observation, we label it as one.

Does this actually help ahead of back-to-school or January?

That's the main use case. Point it at your category and market a few weeks before a seasonal window opens, and Alerts and Patterns show you what competitors are already loading in and which formats are close to worn out, so your first test inside the window isn't the same happy-kid-and-streak clip everyone else is running.

What does it cost?

Connecting is free, and your first report is free. All three feeds run about $250/mo, on a card, cancel anytime. The full loop with finished creative starts at $3K/mo plus a media share. We don't sell one-off scripts or concepts.

What can the MCP do beyond the three feeds?

Read-only covers competitors, patterns, trends, insights, and reports. Brand-write lets you onboard a brand, score concepts through its Lens, and run a full cycle.
