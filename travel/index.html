The shift

Most travel teams brief this quarter's campaigns off last quarter's winners.

A hook peaks in one market, you catch it in the post-mortem, and by then the next cycle is already briefed. Meanwhile a competitor announces five new cities, a hotel chain in 14 markets swaps its whole creative angle, and the demand curve moves before anyone on your side has seen it. Reading the market by hand, one Ad Library tab at a time, does not keep up.

The metric that matters is Time-to-Winner: days from start to a creative that clears your KPI gate and holds in spend. You cut it by putting the signal in front of the brief, in every market at once. Speed compounds. Opinions don't.

Questions

The honest answers.

Is this travel-specific data, or generic?

Travel-specific. You set the category (hotels, airlines, OTA, experiences, car rental) and the market, and everything returned is scoped to it. A hotel hook that wins in the UAE is not the same as one that wins in Germany, and the data reflects that.

What categories and markets do you cover?

Hotels and resorts, airlines, OTAs and booking, experiences, and car rental, across the US, UK, UAE, Australia, Germany, India, Brazil and more. Adding a market takes one paste. If a category or country is thin, tell us and we prioritize it.

How current is the data?

Alerts land the morning a move breaks in the press. Patterns and reports refresh weekly. The live signal below updates monthly. You're reading what's running and holding now, not an annual trend piece.

What exactly are Alerts?

Alerts are press and market moves: a competitor announcing new routes or cities, a hotel group signing a sponsorship, an OTA expanding inventory, a loyalty program relaunching. They come from monitoring the press, not the ad library. They are not ad-creative reports.

Is the data real, or model guesswork?

Real. Live competitor creative across markets, structured and scored. The why on a pattern is grounded in what's actually running and holding in spend, not what a model thinks sounds plausible. Try it below.
