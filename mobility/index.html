The market tax

A new GEO is not a new locale. It is a new creative language.

Most mobility teams brief their first campaign in a new market the way they briefed the last one. Same driver-earnings angle. Same discount frame. Same lifestyle vibe, run through a translator.

Then it underperforms, and nobody can say why until the spend is gone. What wins in Brazil is not what wins in Indonesia. A rideshare app in 12 markets learns that twelve separate times, one wasted flight at a time.

Time-to-Winner is the number of days from starting a market to a creative that clears your KPI gate and holds. Every market you discover by burning budget adds to it. This cuts the discovery cost to reading.

Speed compounds. Opinions don't.

What mobility teams ask first

Does this cover emerging markets?

Yes. LATAM, Southeast Asia, the Middle East and Africa are core coverage, not an afterthought. Most of the creative-language gap that costs you Time-to-Winner shows up exactly in these markets, so that is where the feeds go deepest.

What languages does the creative come in?

Hooks are read in the market's own language, with the reasoning in English. Native Portuguese, Hindi, Bahasa Indonesia and Arabic creative is not a translation of an English idea. Tone, humor and reference points are local, because that is what actually converts.

How often is the data updated?

Patterns and Alerts move continuously as the press and the category move. Reports land weekly. When a competitor makes a move, you see it in Alerts the day it breaks, not the week their spend hits your feed.

Is any of this real, or is it a demo?

Real. Scroll up to the live signal section and read the actual hooks and reasoning by category and market. The gated ones open with an email. What you connect to is the same signal, kept current.

Does it integrate with our attribution tools?

Orcool reads through MCP, so it sits next to whatever your assistant already touches. It informs the brief, it does not replace your attribution stack. You keep measuring winners your way. This tells you which creative to point at each market first.
